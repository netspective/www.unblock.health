Why Unblock Health?

Unblock Health is ready to dismantle barriers standing in the way of real-world interoperability, patient directed access, and transparency of the future.

Demand for real time access of health information

The rise of participatory medicine, shared decision making, and consumerism have contributed to patients expecting and demanding access to their medical records in real-time, where they are.

Streamline communication between end-users

Our asynchronous messaging creates a seamless conduit for communication between end-users. Provide real-time updates, address questions, and reduce staff and patient administrative burden with Unblock Health chat.

Need for trust and transparency

The lack of transparency surrounding patient directed access to their health information creates friction between patients and their providers as well as an unsatisfactory customer experience. Greater transparency into patient directed access processes will build trust.

Improving data quality and reducing errors

With increases in accessing health information comes the discovery of the many errors and omissions that are often found within medical records. Patients need a streamlined way of submitting a medical record correction request for continuity of care purposes. Patients have a right to a comprehensive and accurate medical record. It's a matter of safety.

Rise in the need for patient advocacy tools

As consumers, patients are in demand of patient advocacy tools to pushback against a healthcare ecosystem that is responsible for friction points that hamper their healthcare trajectory.

Make informed decisions about their care

Patients often cannot get access to their health information, especially when they are faced with an emergency or critical diagnosis, such as a chronic illness or cancer.

Improve patient satisfaction & brand reputation

Resolve and reduce delays and pinpoint weaknesses in HIPAA compliant information access workflows that negatively impact the patient experience and patient care. Readily improve outcomes by improving coordination and continuity of patient care.

Community of Practice (CoP)

Stop stressing about the new information sharing landscape and compliance with the Information Blocking Rules. The UH CoP supports professionals with a library of the latest best practices and resources as well as a live peer community to support all your access unmet needs and questions.

Unblock Health is an Absolute MUST for:

Health Information Management (HIM) Professionals

  • How well is your organization embracing and complying with information sharing requirements?
  • Do you know what barriers patients are facing at your organization in accessing their data?
  • Don't have a strategy to support patient directed access?
  • Still handling hand-written or faxed addendum requests for processing and implementing patient record addendums?

Office of Patient Experience

  • Lack of coordination of care negatively impacts the patient experience & outcomes, especially in chronic illness & life altering diagnoses.
  • Delays in access to medical records and health information may result in negative HCAPS results or impact facility/caregiver reputation in public domain.
  • What if your organization's patient information access practices were shared online for patients & consumers to consider when deciding where to get care?

Health Information Exchanges (HIEs) & Qualified Health Information Networks (QHINs)

  • Is supporting patient directed access a priority for your organization?
  • Do you lack subject matter expertise about individual access services?
  • Are you ready to begin a stream-lined approach to individual access services?

Patients, Carepartners, Caregivers, Advocates

  • Are you facing a life-altering diagnosis and can't get access to your own medical information quickly enough?
  • Are you unable to make informed, educated decisions about your care due to information blocking?
  • Have you found an error or omission in your medical record that needs to be corrected immediately for continuity of care purposes?
